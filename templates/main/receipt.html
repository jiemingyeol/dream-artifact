This shop opens for things that were never meant to be stored.
Memories that are too quiet to be shared, too awkward to name, or too
fragile to carry any longer. Here, nothing is repaired or made useful.
What arrives at the counter is gently received, exchanged, and
reshaped, finding a second existence as something lighter, stranger, or
dreamlike. The shop is run slowly, without urgency.
There is no promise of closure, only permission. Kept by one person,
